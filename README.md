# ai slop to learn simulation of condensed matter

## exam questions:

Chapter 1:

\- 1. What are the two fundamental functions we need to know to run MD?

\- 2. Why is Newtonian mechanics relevant for MD?

\- 3. Which observables are conserved in a closed system? Why?

Chapter 2:

\- 4. Name two numerical integration algorithms and their advantages/disadvantages.

\- 5. What are periodic boundary conditions and why are they used in MD quite often?

Chapter 3:

\- 6. What is the most time-consuming step in a naive all-pair evaluation MD? How can it be made

faster?

\- 7. What happens if the integration time-step is a bit too large? What happens if it is much too

large?

\- 8. What is a radial distribution function and what information can be obtained from it?

\- 9. What is the difference between radial distribution functions and Voronoi tessellation?

Chapter 4:

\- 10. Name a few static and dynamic properties that can be obtained from an MD trajectory.

\- 11. Why can we compute dynamic (=non-equilibrium) properties from equilibrium trajectories?

\- 12. What are possible sources of error in analyzing an MD trajectory? How can we avoid them?

Chapter 5:

\- 13. Describe the microcanonical (NVE), canonical (NVT), and isobaric-isothermal (NPT)

ensemble.

\- 14. How is temperature controlled in an MD run? Name at least two options and their

advantages/disadvantages.

\- 15. For computing dynamic properties from an MD run, name one good and bad choice of a

thermostat, and explain your choice.

\- 16. How is pressure controlled in an MD run? Name at least two options and their

advantages/disadvantages.

Chapter 6:

\- 17. Name at least two two-body potentials and what they are used for.

\- 18. What is the general idea of a bond-order potential compared to a regular two-body

potential?

\- 19. What potential terms are needed for studying molecular systems (bonded and non-bonded

terms)?

\- 20. Draw the approximate shape of bond, angle, and dihedral terms in a molecular potential

energy function.

\- 21. Can a bond break in a classical harmonic bond potential? Why/why not?

Chapter 7:

\- 22. How can we transfer classical force field parameters from one molecule to another?

\- 23. To which term in the potential energy function does a cutoff apply?

\- 24. Name a few options for potential cutoffs. What are their advantages/disadvantages?

\- 25. What is Ewald summation used for, and what is its (very general, no details needed)

concept?

\- 26. To what data are force field parameters usually fit to?

Chapter 8:

\- 27. What is the advantage/promise of machine learning potentials?

\- 28. Explain the difference between a fixed and learned descriptor ML potential

\- 29. How are translational and rotational invariance included in ML potentials?

\- 30. How is permutational invariance included in ML potentials?
